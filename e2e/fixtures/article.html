On the quiet art of marginalia

A book that has been read, truly read, is never the same object twice. The reader returns from the page carrying something back with them—a phrase, a doubt, a small bright idea—and they leave a mark in return.

The margins are where the argument happens: a private correspondence between the reader and the text, conducted in pencil. This is the first test paragraph that the Playwright tests will highlight.

The web was supposed to be good at this. Instead we got the highlight as a kind of applause, a way of saying yes, this into the void.

What I want, what I have always wanted, is a thinner layer. One that sits on the page without changing it. One that lets me talk back.
